Noah Singer

I study computer science and math at Harvard (Pforzheimer House '22). My primary areas of interest include complexity theory, operating systems and security, and machine learning. For instance, I have recently been interested in the theoretical foundations of deep learning. I plan to pursue a Ph.D. in computer science after graduating. I also have years of experience with software and web engineering.

I use this site to collect some of my papers and slides (lectures, talks, research, etc.), some old projects, and my current and past coursework with some (handwritten) course notes which might be of interest to future students.

Activities

Work Experience: In Summer 2019, I worked as a software engineering intern at Airbnb in San Francisco on the Host Growth & Traffic team, where I built a production data pipeline to discover and manage large quantities of Google search advertising keywords targeting Airbnb hosts. I was responsible for efficiently scaling up new listing creation due to search ads and generating tens of thousands of dollars in weekly revenue.

Teaching: At Harvard, I am a Teaching Fellow for CS 121: Introduction to Theoretical Computer Science in Fall 2019, organizing guest lectures in the "CS 121.5" recitation section for advanced students. I am also a peer tutor with the Academic Resource Center in computer science and math classes.

Volunteering: I am involved in programs that teach computer science and programming. In Spring 2019, I worked with Harvard's Digital Literacy Project to teach Scratch and Processing.js to Boston-area middle school students. I also taught a seminar entitled "Integer multiplication, Karatsuba's algorithm, and complexity bounds in theoretical computer science" at Harvard Splash 2019 that introduces students to the building blocks of theoretical computer science.

Skills

Programming: I am fluent in Python, have consistently used Java, C/C++, and OCaml, and have enjoyed (or endured?) Haskell, LISP, SQL, JavaScript, x86 assembly, Ruby, and PHP at various points in my life. I have experience with frameworks and software including numpy/scipy, Django, Angular, Bootstrap, Jekyll (which powers this site), LaTeX, Ansible, general UNIX, and git.

Mathematics: I have a strong background in abstract mathematics, including linear algebra, the theory of groups, rings, and modules, topology, measure theory, and complex analysis. I am very comfortable with writing proofs.

High School

I was extremely fortunate to attend the Math, Science, and Computer Science Magnet Program at Montgomery Blair High School in Silver Spring, MD.

Some of my proudest achievements in high school include:

  • Taking classes in topics such as differential equations, complex analysis, mathematical physics, and algorithms and data structures,
  • Building the new version of Silver Chips Online, Montgomery Blair High School's newspaper site and the associated multimedia content management backend,
  • Working with Tom Goldstein at the University of Maryland, College Park in Summer 2017 to implement a Python version of his algorithm FASTA combining various techniques for nonconvex optimization,
  • Captaining the Computer Team and delivering weekly lectures on subjects like complexity theory, CPU architecture, Fourier analysis, and neural networks,
  • Directing ångstromCTF, a free, online cybersecurity competition that drew more than 1,500 teams worldwide in 2018, and
  • Contributing to teams that won awards in mathematics, science, and computer science competitions, such as 1st place at the nationwide High School Forensics competition at NYU's Cybersecurity Awareness Week conference ('17), 2nd place at the University of Maryland's High School Programming Contest ('18), and 1st place at the National Ocean Science Bowl in Boulder, CO ('18).

Other Interests

I love science fiction! My favorite short story is William Gibson's Fragments of a Hologram Rose. Some of my favorite novels are Gibson's Neuromancer, Dan Simmons' Hyperion, and Kim Stanley Robinson's Red Mars. Some other sci-fi favorite authors are Asimov, Dick, Bradbury, and Le Guin; non-sci-fi favorites include Toni Morrison and Haruki Murakami.

I'm also really into music. I listen mostly to electronica, alt rock, dance, and hip hop; my favorite artists include Daft Punk, Ratatat, Gorillaz, Arctic Monkeys, J. Cole, OVERWERK, Kanye West, OutKast, and The Strokes. I also play classical piano in my free time.

When school's not in session, I also spend a lot of time catching up on television and cooking and baking.
